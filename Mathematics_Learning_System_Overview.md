## 1. System Overview

The Mathematics Learning System is a structured, course-specific learning and practice platform built to support university students in core mathematics courses. It functions as an integrated learning environment that combines multiple roles: a **structured learning tool**, a **practice engine**, an **exam preparation platform**, and a **mistake-analysis system**.

The system was built by aggregating and reorganizing all available course materials — lectures, tutorials (תרגולים), homework assignments, and past exams — into a unified, topic-based knowledge base. From this foundation, the system generates organized resources including key theorems and helper claims (טענות עזר), golden rules (כללי זהב), curated exercises sorted by topic, comprehensive lists of definitions, and structured summaries. Critically, the system answers questions and generates content **exclusively based on what was taught in the course**, ensuring full alignment with the curriculum.

---

## 2. Courses Covered

The system currently supports three foundational mathematics courses:

### Calculus (Infinitesimal Calculus / אינפי)
Topics include limits, continuity, derivatives, integrals, sequences, and series. Exercises range from computational problems (calculating limits, derivatives, integrals) to proof-based questions (epsilon-delta proofs, convergence proofs) and conceptual reasoning tasks.

### Discrete Mathematics (מתמטיקה בדידה)
Topics include logic, set theory, relations, functions, combinatorics, graph theory, and proof techniques (induction, contradiction, contrapositive). The system provides structured practice in formal proof writing alongside computational combinatorics and graph problems.

### Linear Algebra (אלגברה לינארית)
Topics include vector spaces, linear transformations, matrices, determinants, eigenvalues and eigenvectors, inner product spaces, and diagonalization. Exercises include both computational work (row reduction, determinant calculation) and theoretical proofs about vector spaces and transformations.

For each course, the system maintains a complete taxonomy of topics, with exercises categorized by subject, difficulty, and type — drawn directly from lectures, homework, and past exams.

## 3. Student Learning Flow

The student experience follows a clear, guided path:

1. **Entry & Course Selection** — The student enters the system and selects one of the three supported courses.
2. **Topic Navigation** — Within each course, the student can browse topics, view organized lists of definitions and theorems, or access golden rules and helper claims relevant to a specific subject.
3. **Practice** — The student selects a topic and receives exercises curated from course materials. These include homework-style problems, past exam questions, and tutorial exercises — all organized by topic rather than by source.
4. **Answer Submission** — The student works through problems and submits their reasoning or solution. The system accepts both final answers and step-by-step work.
5. **Feedback & Learning** — The system provides detailed feedback: correctness evaluation, step-by-step solution guidance, references to relevant theorems and definitions, and explanations rooted specifically in the course material.

---

## 4. Exercise Types

The system includes a diverse range of question formats drawn from actual course materials:

- **Proof Questions** — Formal mathematical proofs using induction, contradiction, epsilon-delta arguments, and direct proof techniques. Students practice constructing rigorous arguments.
- **Computational Problems** — Calculation-based exercises such as solving integrals, performing matrix operations, computing determinants, and evaluating combinatorial expressions.
- **Conceptual Questions** — Questions that test understanding of definitions, relationships between concepts, and the ability to identify when a theorem applies.
- **Step-by-Step Reasoning** — Multi-stage problems where students must show their work through a logical progression, with evaluation at each step.
- **True/False with Justification** — Statements that students must evaluate and either prove or disprove, a format common in Israeli university math exams.

Answer evaluation considers both the final result and the reasoning process, with attention to correct use of definitions, proper application of theorems, and logical structure.

---

## 5. Feedback Mechanism

The system employs a layered feedback approach designed to promote genuine understanding rather than rote memorization:

- **Immediate Correctness Feedback** — Students learn whether their approach and answer are correct.
- **Guided Hints** — When a student struggles, the system offers progressive hints that direct attention to relevant theorems or definitions without giving away the full solution.
- **Step-by-Step Explanations** — Complete worked solutions are available, broken into logical steps with references to the specific course material (lecture, theorem, or definition) that justifies each step.
- **Mistake Analysis** — When errors are identified, the system explains *why* the approach was incorrect, pinpointing the specific misconception or misstep.
- **Retry Mechanism** — Students can reattempt problems after receiving feedback, reinforcing learning through corrected practice.

All feedback is grounded exclusively in the course curriculum — the system never references material outside what was taught.

---

## 6. Learning Logic

The system incorporates several structured learning mechanisms:

- **Topic-Based Organization** — Material is organized by topic rather than by source, allowing students to focus deeply on specific areas of weakness.
- **Structured Learning Paths** — Within each course, topics follow a logical progression that mirrors the course structure, building from foundational concepts to advanced applications.
- **Weakness Targeting** — Students can identify and focus on topics where they need more practice, supported by the organized topic taxonomy.
- **Progressive Difficulty** — Exercises within each topic can be approached from simpler computational problems to more complex proof-based challenges.
- **Comprehensive Review Resources** — Lists of definitions, theorems, helper claims, and golden rules serve as structured review tools that support both targeted practice and broad revision.

---

## 7. Exam Preparation — The 10-Day Marathon

A dedicated 10-day exam preparation marathon was designed and tested as a structured, intensive review program:

- **Structure** — The marathon spans 10 days, with each day focused on specific topics and exercise types. It progresses from foundational review in the early days to full exam simulation in the final days.
- **Daily Tasks** — Each day includes a defined set of activities: reviewing key definitions and theorems, practicing topic-specific exercises, and working through past exam questions. The workload is calibrated to be intensive but sustainable.
- **Progression** — Students move from isolated topic practice (days 1–5) through mixed-topic problem sets (days 6–8) to full timed exam simulations (days 9–10).
- **Testing & Feedback** — The marathon was tested with students who provided feedback on pacing, difficulty progression, and effectiveness. Feedback indicated that the structured daily approach reduced exam anxiety and improved confidence in weak areas.

## 8. Learning Data

The system is designed to capture and reflect meaningful learning data:

- **Success Rate** — Performance tracking across topics, showing which areas a student has mastered and which require further work.
- **Attempt History** — Number of attempts per exercise, reflecting persistence and learning progression.
- **Topic-Level Analysis** — Aggregated difficulty metrics showing which topics students find most challenging.
- **Improvement Tracking** — Ability to observe performance trends over time, particularly useful during marathon-style intensive preparation.

This data serves both the student (for self-assessment) and has potential value for instructors seeking insight into common student difficulties.

---

## 9. Pedagogical Approach

The system is grounded in several established educational principles:

- **Active Problem Solving** — Learning through doing. The system prioritizes practice and application over passive reading.
- **Learning from Mistakes** — Errors are treated as learning opportunities. Detailed feedback on incorrect answers builds deeper understanding than simply providing correct solutions.
- **Structured Revision** — Organized lists of definitions, theorems, and golden rules provide scaffolding that supports efficient review and long-term retention.
- **Curriculum Fidelity** — By restricting all content to what was actually taught in the course, the system ensures that student effort is always directed toward exam-relevant material. This eliminates the common problem of students studying from misaligned external sources.
- **Scaffolded Independence** — The hint system and progressive feedback encourage students to develop their own problem-solving abilities rather than creating dependency on provided solutions.

## 10. Future Potential

The system has significant potential for broader academic application:

- **Official Course Practice Platform** — Integration as a supplementary practice tool for mathematics courses, providing students with organized, curriculum-aligned practice outside of class hours.
- **Exam Preparation System** — The marathon model can be adapted for any exam period, offering structured preparation that reduces student stress and improves outcomes.
- **Student Progress Tracking** — Learning data can inform instructors about common difficulties, enabling targeted intervention and curriculum adjustment.
- **Teaching Support Tool** — Organized topic taxonomies, exercise banks, and definition lists can support teaching assistants and lecturers in creating tutorials and assignments.
- **Scalability** — The methodology of aggregating course materials into a structured, topic-based learning system can be replicated for additional courses and departments.
- **Research Foundation** — The system's data collection capabilities create opportunities for educational research on learning patterns, common misconceptions, and effective practice strategies in university mathematics.

---

## Summary

The Mathematics Learning System transforms scattered course materials — lectures, homework, tutorials, and past exams — into a unified, intelligent learning platform. It gives students organized access to definitions, theorems, golden rules, and topic-sorted exercises, all grounded exclusively in their course curriculum. Through structured practice, detailed feedback, and intensive exam preparation programs, the system addresses a real and persistent challenge in university mathematics education: the gap between attending lectures and being able to independently solve problems. By keeping all content course-specific and providing layered support from hints through full solutions, it meets students where they are and helps them build genuine mathematical competence.
