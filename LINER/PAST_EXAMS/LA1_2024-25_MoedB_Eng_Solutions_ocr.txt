        Prove that AB is symmetric if and only if AB = BA.
        Solution:
        Since A and B are skew symmetric it holds that At = −A and that Bt = −B.
        ⇒: Suppose that AB is symmetric, that is, (AB)t = AB. Then BA = −Bt −At = Bt At = (AB)t = AB.
                                                                                

        ⇐: Suppose that AB = BA. Then (AB)t = Bt At = (−B)(−A) = BA = AB which implies that AB is symmetric.

5.2. (10 points) Let V and W be two finitely generated vector spaces over the field F.
     Suppose that dim V ≥ 1 and that dim Hom (V, W) = 8. Let T : V → W be an injective linear transformation.
     Compute dim ImT .

     Solution:
     As T is an injective linear transformation from V to W, it holds that dim ker T = 0 and that dim W ≥ dim V ≥ 1.
     By the second theorem of dimensions it holds that


                                      dim V = dim ker T + dim ImT = 0 + dim ImT = dim ImT


     By a theorem from the lecture, it holds that dim V · dim W = dim Hom (V, W). Since dim Hom (V, W) = 8 we
     obtain that dim V · dim W = 8, and we consider two cases:

       • Case 1: dim V = 2 and dim W = 4. In this case dim ImT = dim V = 2.
       • Case 2: dim V = 1 and dim W = 8. In this case dim ImT = dim V = 1.

     (If dim V > 1 the second case does not hold and dim ImT = 2.)
